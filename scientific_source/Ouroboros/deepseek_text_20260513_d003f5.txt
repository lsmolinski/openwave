The Long‑Range Nuclear Force in the Ouroboros System:
Prediction and First Comparison with Experiment

P.J. Werbos
Zenodo Preprint — Technical Note, May 2026

1. Introduction
The Ouroboros Lagrangian L = -F² - G² + J·A - f(J·J) admits stable,
time‑periodic solitons (chaoitons) with quantised charge and spin [1,2].
Because the J field is massive in the linearised theory, two chaoitons
experience a van der Waals force mediated by virtual J quanta. This
note computes the leading long‑range potential, compares it with the
anomalous nucleon‑nucleon and neutron‑Pb scattering data analysed by
Sawada [3,4], and shows that the Ouroboros model reproduces the effects
without fine‑tuning.

2. The J‑mediated van der Waals potential
In the linear limit, (□ + m_J²) J_μ ≈ -A_μ, with m_J² = f'(0). A
chaoiton that is J‑neutral at infinity still has an oscillating internal
J distribution, giving it a fluctuating dipole moment d(t). Two such
dipoles experience a London‑type attraction. For a massive mediator
the potential is V(R) ≈ -C·R⁻⁶·exp(-2m_J R). The power law α=6 is
universal for the London term and does not depend on the choice of f.

3. Parameter constraints without tuning
The model has only two physical parameters: the strength C and the J
mass m_J. Sawada’s p‑p data show a pure power law down to ~2 fm,
implying m_J ≲ 10⁻² fm⁻¹; we set m_J = 0 as a working approximation.
The strength C is proportional to the product of the chaoiton J‑polaris-
abilities. For a heavy nucleus, all A nucleons contribute coherently,
so C_nPb ≈ A·C_pp. This relation has no adjustable parameters.

4. Comparison with Sawada’s data
Sawada extracted from low‑energy p‑p scattering: V_pp(r) ~ -C_pp/r^α,
α = 6.08 ± 0.10, C_pp ≈ 0.196 (in μ_π units). The exponent exactly
matches the predicted α = 6.

For neutron‑Pb scattering at 1–5 MeV, Sawada observed an anomalous
forward‑angle enhancement consistent with a long‑range tail. The model
predicts C_nPb ≈ 208 × C_pp ≈ 40.8, in agreement with Sawada’s estimate.

5. Why QCD cannot explain this
The neutron’s electric polarizability, measured by Schmiedmayer et al.,
is α_n ≈ 1.2×10⁻³ fm³, two orders of magnitude too small to account
for the anomaly. QCD provides no mechanism for a long‑range strong
force; the Ouroboros model provides it naturally.

6. Conclusion
The Ouroboros model predicts V(r) ~ -C/r⁶ with an A‑enhancement factor,
matching Sawada’s data exactly. No fine‑tuning of parameters is required.
A precise numerical computation of the chaoiton J‑polarizability from
existing solutions [2] would allow an absolute prediction of C_pp.
