# ANTI-GRAVITY Xperiment

We could create this Xperiment right now, making use of the already developed infrastructure and adding a few more modules to OpenWave.

The key insight: we don't need to simulate Earth's gravitational field using 10^50 planet atoms (intractable for any computer). Instead, we directly apply the Gravitational Force Field that results in the acceleration constant g = 9.80665 m/s² — the same approach your paper uses in Eq. 2.3.5 where E_g = m_p × g × h.

From there, we simulate everything else: the crystal lattice structure, YBCO superconductor unit cell, electron and proton motion, longitudinal waves generated from proton vibration, and the force emerging from those “inverse” waves that counteracts Earth's gravitational field, creating the effect of gravity modulation. With this we might be able to demonstrate that gravity emerges from wave energy density gradients.

I've started making a plan for this project, but I need to hear your thoughts about its feasibility before moving forward.

From this approach, we're not "fighting" gravity by simulating atomic interactions - we're modeling how proton vibration energy can offset gravitational potential energy, which is a much more tractable simulation problem.
