## 1. The Zitterbewegung / charge-quantization exchange

Sulich asked: if a particle can exist without its Zitterbewegung (ZBW), is the ZBW a redundant element that should be phased out?

Duda's answer separates two mechanisms cleanly:

- **What holds the particle together is charge quantization**, not the clock. Gauss's law fixes the charge inside any region unless it crosses the border; the topological version is Gauss-Bonnet. Without charge quantization an electron "would explode into repelling subcharges." The only way to destroy charge is annihilation with the opposite charge.
- **The de Broglie clock (ZBW) is a separate, energy-minimizing mechanism.** Field configurations of particles (especially the electron, and neutrinos which carry 0 charge and so need a different binding mechanism) *prefer* to oscillate because oscillation REDUCES the energy: "these oscillations need to allow to reduce energy, which minimum are the preferred frequencies." Stopping the clock can therefore only RAISE the energy, above the 511 keV energy-minimizing de Broglie state. Interactions can perturb the frequency, slightly increasing energy.

Toymodel reference for the energy-vs-frequency minimum: arXiv:2501.04036 (also the Wolfram Community writeup in [`theory/`](theory/)).
