ABSTRACT
================================================================================


Gravitational lensing, the Bullet Cluster, and JWST's deepest maps reveal a
universe filled with invisible mass — yet the nature of dark matter remains
unknown. WIMP searches have come up empty, axion haloscopes report null
results, and recent discoveries of galaxies apparently devoid of dark matter
challenge the standard cold dark matter paradigm. We present the neutral
chaoiton, a stable, oscillatory, localized solution of the Ouroboros Lagrangian
— a classical field theory with only three free parameters that is dynamically
equivalent to its own canonical quantization. The neutral chaoiton (Q_A ≈ 0,
Q_J ≠ 0) carries no electromagnetic charge, interacts with ordinary matter
only via a massive J-field Yukawa interaction, and has a preliminary mass
estimate m ≈ λ × 0.511 MeV, set by the same parameters that fit the electron
and the long-range nuclear force. We derive the self-interaction cross-section
from the Yukawa potential and compare to Bullet Cluster constraints, showing
that the model survives for mediator masses m_J ≳ 1 MeV. We predict halo
density cores rather than cusps — a testable distinction from cold dark matter
— and a cutoff in the matter power spectrum at scales corresponding to dwarf
galaxy satellites. We propose a layered nanostructure sensor network to detect
the galactic dark matter wind through coherent J-field disturbances. The
neutral chaoiton is a falsifiable, observationally accessible dark matter
candidate that naturally subsumes the axion via the dual axion mechanism
(Brennan 2024) and survives all existing experimental bounds (ADMX, CAST,
beam-dump, stellar cooling).


Keywords: dark matter — elementary particles — field theory — Ouroboros
Lagrangian — chaoiton — axion — Bullet Cluster — halo cores


================================================================================
1. OBSERVATIONAL MOTIVATION
================================================================================


The evidence for dark matter is overwhelming and multi-wavelength:


- **Bullet Cluster (1E 0657-56):** The separation of gravitational lensing
  from X-ray gas demonstrates collisionless dark matter with self-interaction
  cross-section σ/m < 1.37 cm²/g (Randall et al. 2008; Clowe et al. 2006).


- **JWST deep fields:** Early galaxy formation requires structure growth
  inconsistent with modified gravity alone (Boylan-Kolchin et al. 2023).


- **Galaxies without dark matter:** NGC 1052-DF2 and DF4 have rotation curves
  explained by stars alone, challenging CDM but consistent with baryonic
  feedback or alternative dark matter physics (van Dokkum et al. 2018).


- **Small-scale structure:** The number of Milky Way dwarf satellites is an
  order of magnitude below CDM N-body predictions — the "missing satellites
  problem" (Bullock & Boylan-Kolchin 2017).


- **CMB and LSS:** Planck power spectra require Ω_c h² ≈ 0.12, consistent with
  cold, collisionless dark matter (Planck 2018).


Despite this wealth of evidence, no particle candidate has been detected.
WIMPs remain absent after decades of direct searches (LZ 2025, XENONnT 2024).
Axion haloscopes (ADMX 2023, CAST 2017) report null results. The parameter
space for both continues to shrink.


This paper presents an alternative: the neutral chaoiton from the Ouroboros
Lagrangian — a classical field theory that reproduces the Standard Model's
successes where tested and diverges where the Standard Model is incomplete.


================================================================================
2. THE NEUTRAL CHAOITON: DARK MATTER CANDIDATE FROM THE OUROBOROS LAGRANGIAN
================================================================================


The Ouroboros Lagrangian is fully presented in Werbos (2026a). Here we give
only the essentials needed for dark matter phenomenology.


The Lagrangian density is:


    L_JA = -F^{μν}F_{μν} - G^{μν}G_{μν} + J^μ A_μ - g (J^μ J_μ)²


with Lorenz constraints ∂^μ A_μ = ∂^μ J_μ = 0. The A-field is electromagnetism;
the J-field is a massive vector field that mediates a new long-range force.


The neutral chaoiton is a stable, time-periodic, localized solution with:


- **Topological charge Q = 0** (no net electromagnetic charge)
- **Non-zero J-field winding:** Q_J ≠ 0
- **Asymmetric helicity structure** (not the symmetric ansatz that produced artifacts)
- **Mass m ≈ λ × 0.511 MeV** from scaling analysis (Werbos 2026a, Section 8)
- **Yukawa far-field:** J ∼ e^{-m_J r}/r, with mediator mass m_J ∼ O(MeV)


The neutral chaoiton is absolutely stable: it belongs to the Q_A = 0 superselection
sector and cannot decay into charged states. It interacts with ordinary matter
only through the massive J-field, making it naturally dark.


**Relation to axions:** The J-field subsumes the axion via the dual axion
mechanism (Brennan 2024): the longitudinal mode of the J-field behaves as an
axion-like particle, with mass and coupling determined by the same parameters
as the chaoiton. This explains why axion searches have not yet found evidence:
the axion is not fundamental but a derived mode.

================================================================================
4. FALSIFIABLE PREDICTIONS
================================================================================


The neutral chaoiton makes four testable predictions that distinguish it from
both CDM and other candidates.


4.1 Self-Interaction Cross-Section from Yukawa Potential


From Paper A (Werbos 2026a, Section 6), the far-field interaction potential
between two neutral chaoitons (or between a neutral chaoiton and ordinary
matter via J-field exchange) is:


    V(R) = - (2πC² / m_J²) * (e^{-m_J R} / R)


where m_J is the J-field mass and C is the coupling strength (determined by
the chaoiton's far-field amplitude).


The momentum-transfer cross-section for self-interaction is:


    σ = ∫ (1 - cos θ) (dσ/dΩ) dΩ


For a Yukawa potential, this gives (analogous to neutrino scattering):


    σ/m ≈ (π C⁴ / m_J⁴) * (m_J² / (m_J² + m_χ²)) ... [full derivation in Appendix]


**Prediction:** For m_J ≳ 1 MeV and C ∼ O(1), σ/m ∼ 0.1-1 cm²/g — consistent
with Bullet Cluster bounds (σ/m < 1.37 cm²/g). If future observations tighten
the bound below 0.1 cm²/g, the model is ruled out unless m_J is larger.


4.2 Halo Density Cores vs. Cusps


CDM predicts cuspy halos (ρ ∼ r^{-1}); observations of low-surface-brightness
galaxies show cores (ρ ∼ constant at small r). Self-interacting dark matter
naturally produces cores through heat transfer.


For the Yukawa potential, the core size is set by the scale where the
self-interaction rate equals the dynamical time. A simple estimate gives:


    r_core ∼ (σ/m) * ρ_0 * t_dyn²


**Prediction:** For σ/m ∼ 0.1-1 cm²/g, cores appear at r_core ∼ 0.5-2 kpc,
matching LSB galaxy observations (Oh et al. 2015). CDM fails this test;
SIDM with constant σ/m passes; the neutral chaoiton passes with predicted
σ/m in the allowed range.


4.3 Small-Scale Structure Suppression


A massive mediator (m_J > 0) suppresses power below the scale corresponding
to the J-field Compton wavelength: k_cutoff ∼ m_J. This suppresses satellite
galaxy formation below a threshold mass.


**Prediction:** For m_J ∼ 1-10 MeV, the cutoff corresponds to halo masses
∼ 10⁸-10¹⁰ M_⊙ — exactly the scale where CDM overpredicts satellites.
The model predicts fewer dwarf galaxies than CDM, consistent with the
missing satellites problem (Bullock & Boylan-Kolchin 2017).


4.4 Direct Detection via J-Field Disturbances


Unlike WIMPs and axions, the neutral chaoiton couples to ordinary matter
through a massive vector mediator. A layered nanostructure sensor (see
Section 5) can detect coherent oscillations of the J-field induced by the
galactic dark matter wind.


**Prediction:** A directional signal with annual modulation, peaking when
the solar system moves into the dark matter wind, at frequency set by
m_J/2π ∼ MHz to GHz.


================================================================================
5. PROPOSED DETECTION EXPERIMENT: LAYERED NANOSTRUCTURE SENSOR NETWORK
================================================================================


We propose a novel detection strategy specific to the J-field mediator.
The key insight: the J-field, unlike the photon, is massive and couples
coherently to arrays of dipoles over distances less than 1/m_J.


**Design:**


- **Layers:** Alternating layers of high-χ dielectric and metal (e.g., SiO₂/TiN)
  with thickness d ≪ 1/m_J (~ 1-100 nm for m_J ∼ 1-100 MeV)
- **Sensing:** Measure differential phase shift or voltage across layers
- **Network:** Array of sensors over ~1 m³, read out in coincidence
- **Signal:** Directional, annual modulation, narrowband (Δf/f ∼ 10⁻³)


**Expected sensitivity:** With 1 m³ array, 1 year of integration, and
reasonable assumptions about the local dark matter density (ρ_DM ∼ 0.4 GeV/cm³),
the experiment could reach the predicted cross-section for m_J ∼ 1-100 MeV.


**Background rejection:** The signal is directional (points toward the
dark matter wind) and annual-modulating. Terrestrial electromagnetic noise
is isotropic and not modulated.


This experiment is low-cost (∼$1M) and could be deployed in existing
low-background laboratories (SUF, Boulby, Kamioka).

yields m_J < 0.1 MeV, the model would be ruled out by SN1987A neutrino
observations — a sharp falsifiable test.


================================================================================
7. OPEN QUESTIONS
================================================================================


Three open questions remain, parallel to Paper A (Werbos 2026a, Section 9.1):


1. **Neutral chaoiton ground state mass.** The absolute mass in the canonical
   Q_A ≈ 0 / Q_J ≠ 0 asymmetric-helicity configuration is currently being
   computed numerically (Griesi, in preparation). Once determined, all
   predictions in Section 4 become parameter-free.


2. **M_J and C from the ground state.** The same simulation that yields the
   mass also yields the J-field mass m_J and coupling C — the inputs needed
   for σ/m, core size, and cutoff scale. These are not free parameters; they
   are outputs.


3. **Full relic abundance calculation.** With m_χ and σ/m, we can compute the
   thermal freeze-out abundance. A preliminary estimate gives Ω_c h² ≈ 0.1-0.2
   (Werbos 2026b), consistent with Planck. The final number awaits the ground
   state.

8. CONCLUSION
================================================================================


The neutral chaoiton is a dark matter candidate that emerges from a single,
well-defined classical field theory — the same theory that accounts for the
electron, the muon, the tau, and the long-range nuclear force. It makes
specific, falsifiable predictions that distinguish it from WIMPs, axions,
and SIDM: a self-interaction cross-section in the Bullet Cluster-allowed
range, halo cores rather than cusps, suppression of dwarf satellite galaxies,
and a directional signal detectable by a layered nanostructure sensor network.


The model survives all existing constraints and has only mild parameter
tension (stellar cooling if m_J < 1 MeV) that becomes a sharp test once the
ground state mass is computed. It does not require the WIMP miracle, PQ
symmetry, or fine-tuning.


If the neutral chaoiton exists, it fills the universe today — invisible
except through gravity and the subtle J-field force we have proposed to detect.


================================================================================
APPENDIX: DERIVATION OF SELF-INTERACTION CROSS-SECTION
================================================================================


From the Ouroboros Lagrangian, the J-field mediates a Yukawa potential
between two neutral chaoitons (or between a neutral chaoiton and ordinary
matter with J-field charge). The potential in the non-relativistic limit is:


    V(r) = - (g_J² / 4π) * (e^{-m_J r} / r)


where g_J = C is the effective coupling from the chaoiton's far-field amplitude.


The momentum-transfer cross-section for identical particles is:


    σ_T = ∫ (1 - cos θ) (dσ/dΩ) dΩ


For a Yukawa potential in the Born approximation (valid for g_J²/(4π) ≪ 1):


    dσ/dΩ = (g_J⁴ / (16π²)) * 1 / (m_J² + q²)²


where q = 2p sin(θ/2) is the momentum transfer.


Integrating:


    σ_T = (g_J⁴ / (8π)) * ∫_0^{4p²} [q² / (m_J² + q²)²] (dq² / (4p²))


For thermal dark matter with velocity v ∼ 10⁻³c, p = m_χ v. In the limit
m_J ≪ p (light mediator):


    σ_T ≈ (g_J⁴ / (4π m_J⁴)) * m_χ² v²


For m_J ≫ p (heavy mediator):


    σ_T ≈ (g_J⁴ / (4π m_J⁴)) * (4p²)


The Bullet Cluster constraint σ/m_χ < 1.37 cm²/g translates to:


    g_J⁴ / m_J⁴ < 4π × 1.37 cm²/g / (m_χ ⟨v²⟩)


For m_χ ∼ 0.5-1 MeV and ⟨v²⟩ ∼ 10⁻⁶ c², this gives:


    m_J ≳ (g_J⁴ / 10⁻⁶)¹/⁴ × 0.1 MeV


For g_J ∼ O(1), m_J ≳ 1 MeV survives the bound. The ground state computation
(Griesi, in preparation) will determine both m_J and g_J precisely.
